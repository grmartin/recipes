## Conversion Reference

Rounded for kitchen use, not laboratory precision. Teaspoons and tablespoons are the same size in all three systems (modern UK recipes use the metric 5 mL / 15 mL standard rather than the old Imperial spoon sizes); US and Imperial volumes diverge starting at the fluid ounce because the two gallons are different sizes.

### Volume

| Metric Volume | US Customary Volume |  UK Customary Volume |
|--------------:|--------------------:|---------------------:|
|       1.25 mL |               ¼ tsp |                ¼ tsp |
|        2.5 mL |               ½ tsp |                ½ tsp |
|          5 mL |               1 tsp |                1 tsp |
|         15 mL |      1 tbsp (3 tsp) |               1 tbsp |
|         30 mL |    1 fl oz (2 tbsp) |              1 fl oz |
|         60 mL |     ¼ cup (2 fl oz) |              2 fl oz |
|         80 mL |               ⅓ cup |             2¾ fl oz |
|        120 mL |     ½ cup (4 fl oz) |              4 fl oz |
|        160 mL |               ⅔ cup |             5½ fl oz |
|        180 mL |     ¾ cup (6 fl oz) |             6¼ fl oz |
|        240 mL |     1 cup (8 fl oz) |             8½ fl oz |
|        475 mL |     1 pint (2 cups) |   ⅚ pint (16¾ fl oz) |
|        950 mL |    1 quart (4 cups) |             1⅔ pints |
|         3.8 L |  1 gallon (16 cups) | 6¾ pints (3⅓ quarts) |

US and UK Customary mass units are the same avoirdupois ounces and pounds, so those two columns always match; only volume units (fluid ounce, pint, quart, gallon) differ between the two.
